Payment Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026 · ငွေပေးချေမှု မူဝါဒ

This Payment Policy explains how fees are paid for consultations, quotations, and engagements with Shwe Lone Myanmar. Booking rules are detailed in our Consultation Policy and Service Terms & Conditions.

Shwe Lone Myanmar · Myat Noe Oo · Corporate Lawyer

1. General Payment Terms

All professional fees and charges for legal services provided by Myat Noe Oo shall be determined based on the nature, complexity, scope, and requirements of each legal matter.

The applicable fees will be communicated to the Client through a quotation, proposal, engagement letter, or written confirmation before commencement of services.

2. Advance Payment Requirement

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, legal services shall commence only after receipt of the required advance payment.

Advance payment may be required to cover:

  • Consultation fees;
  • Professional service fees;
  • Document preparation fees;
  • Retainer fees;
  • Other agreed charges.

3. Consultation Fee Payment

Consultation appointments are confirmed only after payment of the applicable consultation fee.

Consultation fees are charged based on:

  • Duration of consultation;
  • Complexity of the legal matter;
  • Level of preparation required.

See also our Consultation Policy and published consultation sessions.

4. Professional Service Fees

Professional fees for legal services may be structured as:

  • Fixed Fees;
  • Hourly Rates;
  • Retainer Fees;
  • Project-Based Fees;
  • Other fee arrangements agreed between the Lawyer and Client.

The applicable fee structure will be clearly stated before the engagement begins.

5. Additional Expenses and Third-Party Costs

Unless otherwise stated, professional fees do not include external expenses such as:

  • Government fees;
  • Registration fees;
  • Stamp duties;
  • Notarial fees;
  • Translation fees;
  • Courier charges;
  • Travel expenses;
  • Other third-party costs.

Such expenses shall be paid separately by the Client.

6. Invoices and Receipts

Official invoices and receipts will be issued upon receipt of payment in accordance with applicable accounting and professional requirements.

Clients are responsible for ensuring that payment information provided is accurate.

7. Late Payment

Where payment is overdue, the Lawyer reserves the right to:

  • Suspend ongoing services;
  • Delay delivery of work products;
  • Decline further services until outstanding payments are settled.

8. Cancellation and Refund Policy

Professional fees paid for services that have already commenced are generally non-refundable.

For cancellations before commencement of services, refund eligibility will be considered based on:

  • Work already performed;
  • Administrative costs incurred;
  • Applicable circumstances.

Any refund shall be subject to the Lawyer’s review and applicable agreements.

9. Payment Methods

Payments may be made through approved payment methods provided by the Lawyer.

Payment details will be provided through official communication channels.

10. Tax and Regulatory Obligations

Clients shall be responsible for any applicable taxes, duties, or regulatory charges relating to payments unless otherwise agreed.

11. Changes to Payment Policy

This Payment Policy may be updated from time to time.

The latest version published on this website shall apply to future engagements.
