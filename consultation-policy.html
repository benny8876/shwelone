Consultation Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026 · ဥပဒေဆိုင်ရာ တိုင်ပင်ဆွေးနွေးမှု မူဝါဒ

This Consultation Policy explains how legal consultations are booked, scoped, and confirmed with Shwe Lone Myanmar. Payment rules are detailed in our Payment Policy. Full engagement terms are in Service Terms & Conditions.

Shwe Lone Myanmar · Myat Noe Oo · Corporate Lawyer

1. Purpose of Consultation

Legal consultation is provided to understand your legal concerns, review relevant information, and provide preliminary professional guidance based on the facts and circumstances presented.

Consultation is intended to help clients understand available legal options, potential risks, and appropriate next steps.

2. Booking a Consultation

Consultation appointments must be scheduled in advance through our official communication channels.

Clients may request a consultation through:

  • Website inquiry form
  • Email
  • Telephone
  • Official messaging platforms

Appointment availability is subject to the Lawyer’s schedule.

3. Consultation Fee

Consultation fees are charged based on the nature, complexity, and duration of the consultation.

The applicable consultation fee shall be communicated before the appointment is confirmed.

Payment must be completed according to the payment terms provided. See also our Payment Policy and published consultation sessions.

4. Scope of Consultation

A consultation may include:

  • Understanding the client’s legal concerns;
  • Reviewing preliminary facts and documents;
  • Providing legal opinions or general guidance;
  • Explaining possible legal options and risks.

Unless separately engaged, consultation does not include:

  • Drafting legal documents;
  • Negotiation with third parties;
  • Court representation;
  • Detailed legal research;
  • Continuous legal advisory services.

5. Documents and Information

Clients are encouraged to provide complete and accurate information and relevant documents before or during the consultation.

The quality of legal advice depends on the accuracy and completeness of information provided by the client.

6. Confidentiality

Information shared during consultation will be treated as confidential in accordance with professional obligations.

However, a consultation alone does not automatically establish a lawyer–client relationship unless formally accepted through an engagement process.

7. Cancellation and Rescheduling

Clients should provide reasonable notice if they wish to cancel or reschedule an appointment.

Late cancellation or failure to attend a scheduled consultation may result in the consultation fee being non-refundable.

8. No Guarantee of Outcome

Consultation provides professional guidance based on available information.

No guarantee is made regarding the outcome of any legal matter, transaction, dispute, or proceeding.

9. Establishment of Lawyer–Client Relationship

A lawyer–client relationship is established only after:

  • the engagement has been formally accepted;
  • the scope of services has been agreed; and
  • the required documentation has been completed.

Client သည် တိကျပြည့်စုံသော အချက်အလက်များနှင့် သက်ဆိုင်ရာ စာရွက်စာတမ်းများကို ပေးအပ်ရန် လိုအပ်ပါသည်။

သို့သော် Consultation ပြုလုပ်ခြင်းတစ်ခုတည်းဖြင့် Lawyer–Client Relationship အလိုအလျောက် ဖြစ်ပေါ်မည် မဟုတ်ပါ။

(၇) Appointment ဖျက်သိမ်းခြင်းနှင့် ပြန်လည်ချိန်းဆိုခြင်း
