Why due diligence matters before you buy land

Before you pay a deposit or sign, ownership, encumbrances, and documents should be checked carefully.

Buying land or property involves more than a signed contract. A contract alone does not guarantee clean title or the seller’s right to sell.

What due diligence often covers

Typical checks include ownership verification, the seller’s authority to sell, mortgages or other encumbrances, and a review of key documents and agreements.

Why timing matters

Due diligence is most useful before deposit. Problems found after payment can be harder and costlier to unwind.
